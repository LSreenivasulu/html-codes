It appears you are asking for information about Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, often referred to as "Abdhul Kalam." He was a renowned Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Here's information about him and his contributions to the world, including his writings: Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam: A Profile Full Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam Born: October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. Education: Specialized in Aeronautical Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology. Career Highlights: "Missile Man of India": He played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts. He made significant contributions as Project Director to develop India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully injected the Rohini satellite into orbit in July 1980. He was also instrumental in the development of ballistic missiles like Agni and Prithvi under the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP). Chief Scientific Adviser: Served as the Scientific Adviser to the Defence Minister and Secretary, Department of Defence Research & Development from July 1992 to December 1999. During this period, he led the weaponization of strategic missile systems and the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which made India a nuclear weapon state. Principal Scientific Advisor: Served as the Principal Scientific Advisor to the Government of India from November 1999 to November 2001. 11th President of India: Became the 11th President of India on July 25, 2002, serving until July 25, 2007. He was widely known as the "People's President" due to his approachable nature and dedication to national development, particularly focusing on igniting young minds. Awards and Honors: He received India's highest civilian awards, including the Padma Bhushan (1981), Padma Vibhushan (1990), and the Bharat Ratna (1997). He also received numerous honorary doctorates from universities in India and abroad. Demise: He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong. "Written of World Information" - His Literary Works and Vision: Dr. Kalam was a prolific writer and his books have gained wide recognition, not only in India but also globally, for their inspiring messages, scientific insights, and vision for a developed India. His writings often transcended geographical boundaries due to their universal themes of innovation, perseverance, and the power of human potential. Some of his notable books include: "Wings of Fire" (1999): An autobiography that chronicles his early life, struggles, and journey to becoming a prominent scientist and the President of India. It has inspired millions with its message of hard work and resilience. "India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium" (1998): This book outlines his vision for transforming India into a developed nation by the year 2020, focusing on various sectors like economy, technology, and education. "Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power Within India" (2002): In this book, he explores the potential of young minds and encourages them to contribute to national development. "My Journey: Transforming Dreams into Actions" (2013): Shares his experiences and insights on how to achieve one's dreams. "Indomitable Spirit" (2013): A collection of his thoughts and experiences on how to face challenges with an unyielding spirit. "Target 3 Billion" (2011): Co-authored, this book discusses sustainable development and providing urban amenities to rural areas (PURA concept).
